Beaufort, SC: History

Beaufort, South Carolina is a historic Antebellum city in Coastal South Carolina. The city boasts of “streets framed by natural foliage and centuries-old, moss-draped live oak trees.” The city is primarily known for its Civil War-era homes and waterfront views. TripAdivsor promises that “with a pristine coastline, a rich antebellum history and a charming, historic downtown, Beaufort is more than one of the most enriching getaways in South Carolina.”

Of particular interest to the reunion is the fact that Mae Fortune Barber, the wife of Jesse Belmont Barber, Sr. (p. 50), used to teach English in South Carolina. She would somtimes speak to her grandchildren in Geechee, which is the name of both the Gullah people and their language. Beaufort is part of the Gullah "heritage corridor" and the Geechee people work with the Penn Center to preserve their heritage.

Beaufort: Attractions and Tour

You can view a full list of the attractions that Beaufort offers here. However, this year we’ve chosen to go back to our tradition of doing a city tour together. Joye has secured a reservation at the Penn Center at 10:00 AM for 30 people. The guided Grounds Walking Tour is $10 per person. The self-guided tour is free, but admission to the museum is not.

The tour is approximately 1.5 hours long. It can involve either walking or driving. However, there are no stairs to climb in the museum or hills on the campus, for those with mpbility issues. Stay tuned for more info as plans are finalized!
